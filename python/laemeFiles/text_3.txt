{=The scribe has the same somewhat variable shape for thorn, wynn and\n
(rarely) Y. The variability is manifested in a cline of shapes,\n
clearly thorn-shaped and wynn-shaped at each end but often either\n
interchanged or indeterminate. Wynn and Y are almost always dotted,\n
thorn only very occasionally. I have noted when it is dotted. The\n
letters are transcribed as far as possible according to scribal\n
intention judged by context. Note that the abbrevation for yer\n
"there" and y~ "that" (not used in language 1) are identical in\n
this hand. See John Scahill, \'Abbreviations in the Orthographies\n
of The Owl and the Nightingale and their Textual Implications\',\n
Notes and Queries 240 (1995), 426-8. Since the abbreviation mark\n
not only does not go through the ascender of thorn as y~ would\n
normally imply, but does not even touch it, I have realised the\n
forms for both "that" and "there" as yer. Note that a similar\n
abbreviation in hand A of Royal 17.A.xxvii does touch the ascender\n
(though does not bisect it) and does not appear to be used for\n
"there". In Royal the line indicating d rather than D similarly\n
attaches to the right of the ascender rather than bisecting it.\n
In that MS I therefore use y~ to indicate the abbrevated form of\n
"that" although it might be argued that the shape is hardly\n
different from the C scribe\'s form transcribed as yer. (Cf. a\n
similar situation in hand B of Layamon A.) Compare also the\n
abbreviation for "through" found here, as thorn with a line\n
through the descender. This abbreviation is found also in the work of\n
the  Worcester Tremulous Hand, and in BL Royal 17 A xxvii, hands A, B\n
and C and in BL Cotton Nero A. xiv both hands=}\n
